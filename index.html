ELECTRIC SKATEBOARD | C/Assembly

Summary

I used a longboard along with a micro-controller (MSP430 from Texas instruments) to program a force- activaded electric skateboard. The board consisted of two force sensors on top of the board, a motor attached to the back wheels with lasercut parts, and a battery pack to power the electronics. As the back force sensor was pressed harder than the front, the difference in force would be picked up by the micro-controller, thus sending a signal that would ramp the thrust to the back wheels.

The difference in force was then analyzed by the microcontroller, which would control the thrust optimally to ensure the best experience possible, using feedback from the sensors. The signal was then passed as an AC voltage to a single brushless motor applying torque to a single wheel, but the project was easily adaptable to a second motor with a bit more time.

Materials

Texas Instruments MSP430, Force Sensors, AC Brushless Motor, Lasercut Parts, Battery Pack, Electrical Wire.
